MTG Card Voting Tool for Commander

Welcome to the community-driven power level voting tool for Commander (EDH). This tool empowers the community to interpret and vote on the power levels of Magic: The Gathering cards in Commander, based on how they impact the game and the overall experience.

This system is designed to give players a way to assign power levels to cards based on their impact, rather than relying solely on predefined categories. Below, you'll find descriptions of each power level to help guide your voting.

Power Levels for Commander

Power Level 1: Exhibition

Ultra-Casual: These are cards you’ll see in almost any game. They’re often thematic, fun, and not focused on winning. Think of them as the foundation of casual, exhibition-style games where the goal is to enjoy the experience rather than dominate.

Power Level 2: Core

Precon-Level: These cards are the backbone of most preconstructed decks. They’re solid, reliable, and form the foundation of many decks. While they’re not game-winners on their own, they’re essential for building a functional deck. Examples include cards like Sol Ring or Cultivate.

Power Level 3: Upgraded

Optimized Play: These are cards that take a deck to the next level. They’re often better versions of cards found in precons and are included with a specific game plan in mind. They’re powerful but not overwhelming, and they allow decks to run more smoothly without completely dominating the game.

Power Level 4: Optimized

High-Power Play: These cards are highly impactful and can swing games in a major way. They often demand immediate answers and can win games outright if left unchecked. They’re the kind of cards you’d expect to see in high-powered games, where players are looking for strong, synergistic strategies.

Power Level 5: cEDH

Competitive Play: These cards are the backbone of competitive Commander (cEDH) decks. They’re designed to win games as efficiently as possible, often through combos, fast mana, or overwhelming card advantage. While they’re still part of Commander, they’re focused on the metagame and tournament play.
